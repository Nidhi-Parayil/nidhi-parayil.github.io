Nidhi Honey Parayil

PhD student QUT Centre for Robotics

I am Nidhi, a robotics PhD student and my research focuses on developing a reactive policy to navigate a robotic arm through a deformable environment. I am interested in developing techology for sustainable agricultural application. My current research focuses on enabling robots to have physical interaction with deformable objects in the environment (such as pushing through leaves and branches to harvest fruit). In my free time I enjoy reading classic literature, long distance cycling (hoping to do more bikepacking) and social dancing.
